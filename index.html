About Us



Who?

    First Draft is a UChicago-based, non-profit organization that seeks to bring after-school mentorship programs in creative writing to high schools in underprivileged areas of Chicago. In addition to the program, this website is an online publication that allows all of our students to publish their works and let others hear their voices.
    Every member and mentor of The First Draft are University of Chicago undergraduate students who want to make a difference in their community. Our mentors come from diverse backgrounds, and all of them have something different to bring to the classroom from their diverse experiences and passions.

How?

    We send mentors to schools with whom we partner in different areas of Chicago. Based on the students, their experiences with writing, and the school, we design a curriculum that would fit the specific student body the best. We work closely with the partnering schools to make sure that the students and the school can have the best experience possible with us. Our mentors give hands-on guidance to the students and serve as resources for the young writers to put their thoughts on paper.

Why?

    Although writing is one of the most important skills anyone could have, especially for students who are aiming for higher education, it is often heavily undertaught in public school systems around the country. We want to help fill that need for students who want to become better writers, voice their opinions, or turn their creativity into a presentable form. Many teenagers have their own stories to tell, but they may lack the resources to tell them or don’t know how to express them. Our goal is to help them tell their stories.

Mission:

    To provide a space and an opportunity for students of all ages in the underprivileged areas of Chicago to express their ideas through creative writing and foster a passion for reading and writing fiction.
